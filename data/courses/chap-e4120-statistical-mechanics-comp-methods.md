## Why This Course Matters

The statistical-mechanics foundation under all molecular simulation, taken as an
actual graduate Chemical Engineering (CHAP) course. Covers Boltzmann entropy, the
Helmholtz and Gibbs free energies, and the grand potential, then the computational
methods that matter most: Monte Carlo and molecular dynamics for molecular systems.

Two reasons it belongs here:
- It closes the biggest gap in the plan — stat mech is what makes molecular
  simulation make sense, and PChem only grazes it. This course also gives a first
  real pass at MD and Monte Carlo, the simulation craft usually learned only in a lab.
- As a SEAS graduate ChemE course on a Chemical Physics transcript, it signals the
  ability to handle chemical-engineering-level work — relevant for ChemE PhD admissions.

Placed in Sem VII (senior fall): light enough to protect PhD-application time, and
listed as in-progress when applications go out, which is itself a signal.

Open item: confirm with the advisor whether it counts toward the Chemical Physics

Prereq note: assumes "some familiarity with Python." Formal CS here is in Java
(COMS W1004, W3134); Python comes from the ML / Deep Learning courses and self-study.
Make sure Python is solid before taking it.
